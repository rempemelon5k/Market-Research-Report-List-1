<p><strong>The Peptide Antibiotics Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Ribosomal Synthesized Peptide Antibiotics</li><li>Non-Ribosomally Synthesized Peptide Antibiotics</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The peptide antibiotics market includes two main types: ribosomally synthesized and non-ribosomally synthesized peptide antibiotics. Ribosomal synthesized antibiotics are produced by ribosomes and generally consist of amino acids linked in specific sequences, with common examples being certain beta-lactams. In contrast, non-ribosomally synthesized antibiotics are created by specialized enzyme systems, allowing for a greater diversity in structure and function, exemplified by compounds like vancomycin. Both types play crucial roles in combating bacterial infections, particularly multidrug-resistant strains.</p></p>
